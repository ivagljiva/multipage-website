Projects

Several of my course projects have been relevant to my research interests in genomics and computational science. I've used every academic opportunity to gain experience or knowledge that will help me in my research. For instance, I'm hoping to apply the machine learning and data science skills I learned in CS classes to future genome analysis projects. Recently, I participated in the Student Cluster Competition at SC17 to learn more about high-performance computing, particularly in the context of scientific applications. I'll be able to adapt the genomics software I write to HPC settings because of this experience.

Student Cluster Competition 2017

I attended the Student Cluster Competition at SC17, as part of the Illinois Institute of Technology team. We spent several months preparing for the competition by selecting and setting up our cluster hardware and software, profiling the competition applications, and studying the science behind each application. Due to my genomics background, I was the team member responsible for MrBayes, a software for Bayesian phylogenetic inference. We finished as the 6th place team (out of 16).
